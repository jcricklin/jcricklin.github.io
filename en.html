My Thesis

Dynamic Prediction and Optimization Models for Pricing Strategies: Application to Retail Distribution

Defended on February 9, 2026 CY Paris Université ETIS Laboratory (CNRS)

The retail industry is undergoing a major transformation, marked by intensifying competition and margin erosion. This thesis proposes a three-part methodological framework to improve sales forecasting and optimize pricing strategies, combining machine learning, causal discovery and graph-based modeling.

01

Rigorous Forecasting Model Selection

Local univariate models

  • Hyperparameter optimization via TPE (Optuna)
  • Comparison: XGBoost, LightGBM, TCN, TFT, ARIMA
  • Retraining strategy based on the Wilcoxon test
  • Boosting models dominate with shallow trees
Method deployed industrially at Booper
02

Identification of Common Causal Drivers

Causal clustering for global models

  • Causal discovery via PCMCI and Conditional Mutual Information
  • Dimensionality reduction through PCA
  • Geometric clustering with SVM separability criterion
  • Grouping by causal mechanisms, not visual similarity
Better interpretability of global models
03

Modeling Competitive Dynamics

GNN & market share forecasting

  • Causal graphs via ChronoEpilogi / ARDL
  • Stable community detection via MC-Louvain
  • CA-GNN architecture (Community-Aware GNN)
  • Market share forecasting via Wide & Deep + Attention
Superior on ranking metrics (Spearman, NDCG)

Key Findings

Influence Asymmetry

Influential products act through signal aggregation (high in-degree) rather than diffusion

Structure ≠ Economics

GNN embeddings capture competitive topology but correlate weakly with economic variables

Community Amplification

Sales spikes propagate with higher probability within detected communities
